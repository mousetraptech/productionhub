Purpose
This manual defines the complete Manager on Duty (MOD) workflow at the Draylen Mason Music Studio. It serves two functions:

1. Human reference: A step-by-step operational guide that any trained MOD can follow to run a professional event, regardless of technical background.
2. Machine knowledge base: The foundational dataset for Booth Brain, the intelligent booth assistant that will translate natural language into system commands via Production Hub.

The design principle is simple: every piece of operational knowledge that lives in Dave‚Äôs head needs to live in this document. If Dave gets hit by a bus, this manual and Production Hub should be enough for a competent person to run a show.

Decision Boundaries
A core function of this manual is defining what the MOD handles independently vs. what requires escalation. Booth Brain must understand these boundaries to avoid guiding a MOD into territory beyond their training.

‚Ä¢ Simple wireless mic setup (1‚Äì2 handhelds or lavs from charging station)
‚Ä¢ Camera preset recall (PTZ to pre-programmed positions)
‚Ä¢ OBS scene switching and recording start/stop
‚Ä¢ QLab cue firing on showcaller‚Äôs command
‚Ä¢ Projection routing (laptop to switcher to projector)

‚Ä¢ Live audio mixing during shows
‚Ä¢ Significant cable runs or cable strike
‚Ä¢ Any audio troubleshooting beyond ‚Äúcheck battery, check mute, check channel‚Äù

Where AI Helps vs. Where a Checklist Is Fine
Not every step needs intelligence. This section maps each phase to the right tool.

Checklist Is Fine
Steps 1‚Äì6 (facility open) are pure procedure. A laminated card on the wall handles this. Booth Brain adds no value here except maybe reminding a new MOD what‚Äôs next if they ask.

Steps 7‚Äì10 (power up and recall scene) are procedure with one decision point: which Avantis scene. Booth Brain can help here by reading PlanningPod and suggesting the correct scene, but a printed scene-to-event-type matrix on the wall works too.
